## Projects & Research Highlights

### **PRIN 2022 GAIT: Wearable Robotics for Human Motion**
The **PRIN 2022 GAIT** project, hosted at **Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna**, is a comprehensive research initiative focused on the advancement of wearable robotic technologies designed to assist and enhance human locomotion. This project investigates innovative mechatronic designs and robust control strategies to improve gait rehabilitation and mobility for individuals with motor impairments. By integrating cutting-edge sensing technologies with adaptive actuation, the research aims to bridge the gap between robotic assistance and natural human biomechanics. Furthermore, the initiative explores the synergy between human-robot interaction and physiological feedback to ensure seamless and intuitive gait support. Ultimately, the project seeks to deliver high-performance engineering solutions that can be translated into real-world clinical and assistive applications.
* 🌐 [Project Official Website](https://www.santannapisa.it/it/prin-2022-gait)

### **E-Bike Remote Controller: CAD Design & Mechanical Analysis**
During my time at **[Zehus S.r.l.](https://www.zehus.it/)** (the e-mobility division of **[Eldor Corporation](https://www.eldorgroup.com/en/home/)**), I was responsible for the complete mechanical development of an innovative e-bike remote controller. This project involved comprehensive Computer-Aided Design (**CAD**) using advanced surface modeling techniques to ensure both ergonomic functionality and aesthetic integration. Furthermore, I performed detailed analysis for material selection and structural validation, optimizing the design for durability and weight. The workflow extended to Computer-Aided Manufacturing (**CAM**) strategies, where I bridged the gap between design and production by defining manufacturing processes and tolerancing. This experience refined my skills in mechatronic integration, leveraging Eldor’s state-of-the-art industrialization capabilities.
